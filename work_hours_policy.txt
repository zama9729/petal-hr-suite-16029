Work Hours Adherence Policy (Sample)

1. Purpose
This policy establishes guidelines for maintaining punctuality, consistent attendance, and adherence to designated working hours to ensure operational efficiency and fairness across the organization.

2. Scope
This policy applies to all full-time, part-time, and contract employees of the organization, irrespective of role or department.

3. Standard Working Hours

Regular working hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday.

A 1-hour lunch break is permitted between 1:00 PM and 2:00 PM.

Employees are expected to maintain a minimum of 8 hours of productive work per day.

Flexible or shift timings may be approved in writing by the reporting manager or HR.

4. Attendance and Punctuality

Employees must log in and out through the companyâ€™s attendance system each working day.

Repeated late arrivals (more than 3 times in a month) may lead to a written warning.

Absence without prior approval will be marked as unpaid leave or leave without pay (LWP).

5. Breaks and Personal Time

Short breaks totaling up to 15 minutes in the morning and 15 minutes in the afternoon are permitted.

Excessive personal breaks, extended lunch hours, or frequent time away from workstations will be reviewed by HR.

6. Work-from-Home and Remote Hours

Employees on remote work schedules must remain reachable during official hours and log daily work updates.

Failure to maintain communication or consistent work output may lead to revocation of remote privileges.

7. Exceptions and Flexibility

Exceptions may be granted for medical, personal, or family emergencies upon manager and HR approval.

Any long-term flexible arrangements must be documented and approved in writing.

8. Non-Compliance and Disciplinary Action

Persistent violations such as chronic tardiness, unapproved absences, or early departures may result in:

Written warnings

Pay deductions for lost hours

Suspension or termination for repeated offenses

9. Policy Review
This policy is reviewed annually by the HR Department to ensure alignment with operational and legal requirements.
